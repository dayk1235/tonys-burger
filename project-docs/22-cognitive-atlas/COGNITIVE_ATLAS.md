## Preamble

This document is the master navigation layer of the entire Restaurant OS cognitive architecture.

It is not an engine. It is not a constitutional document. It is not a specification. It is not a design language. It is not a philosophy. It is not a system. It is the map of how every constitutional document, every engine, every philosophy, every subsystem, and every business domain relate to each other.

This document is the "map of the brain."

A large cognitive system cannot be understood by reading its documents in sequence. The relationships are too many. The dependencies are too intricate. The concepts are too interconnected. Without a map, every reader — human or agent — will get lost.

This atlas solves that problem. It provides:

- **Topology** — The spatial arrangement of every cognitive component.
- **Dependencies** — How every document and engine depends on every other.
- **Navigation** — How to find any concept, engine, or business domain.
- **Semantics** — What every concept means and where it appears.
- **Flow** — How cognition moves through the system.
- **Cross-references** — Every document explicitly referencing every related document.

The atlas is not a replacement for reading the source documents. It is a prerequisite. A reader who understands the atlas can navigate the source documents with purpose. A reader who does not understand the atlas will be lost in the details.

## Part 1 — Purpose of the Atlas

### 1.1 Why Large Cognitive Systems Require Navigation

Restaurant OS has grown beyond what any single document can describe. The system now includes:

- 18 cognitive engines (existing and planned).
- 15+ constitutional and philosophical documents.
- 2,000+ invariants across all engines.
- 200+ constitutional principles across all domains.
- 100+ canonical relationships between engines.
- 50+ business concepts with cognitive mappings.
- 30+ document modules in the project documentation.

No human and no agent can hold all of this in working memory. Navigation is not a convenience — it is a necessity.

Without navigation:

- **Documents are rediscovered** — Every agent must search for what already exists.
- **Relationships are forgotten** — The connections between documents decay over time.
- **Dependencies are violated** — New work contradicts established decisions.
- **Concepts are duplicated** — The same concept appears in multiple places with different meanings.
- **Knowledge is lost** — What the system knows about itself degrades.

The atlas preserves cognitive coherence at scale.

### 1.2 Why Documentation Is Not Enough

Documentation answers "what" and "why." The atlas answers "where" and "how connected."

Documentation is:
- Vertical — Each document explains its own domain in depth.
- Linear — Documents are read in sequence.
- Isolated — Each document focuses on its own subject.
- Static — Documents change independently.

The atlas is:
- Horizontal — It shows relationships across all domains.
- Non-linear — Readers enter at any point and find their way.
- Connected — Every entry explicitly links to related entries.
- Dynamic — The atlas reflects the current state of all documents.

Documentation without an atlas is a library without a catalog. Every book exists, but none can be found.

### 1.3 Critical Distinctions

**Document** — A single file containing canonical specification of a domain. Example: COGNITIVE_ARCHITECTURE.md, OBSERVATION_SYSTEM.md.

**Knowledge** — The understanding held by the cognitive system about itself and the restaurant. Distributed across all engines.

**Navigation** — The ability to find any concept, document, engine, or relationship on demand. Provided by the atlas.

**Topology** — The spatial arrangement of cognitive components. Which engines are upstream of which. Which documents depend on which.

**Atlas** — The complete navigation system. A collection of maps, graphs, cross-references, and semantic coordinates.

**Map** — A visualisation of one aspect of the topology. Example: the Constitution Dependency Graph, the Engine Communication Graph.

**Dependency** — A directional relationship where one component requires another to function. Engine A depends on Engine B if A requires B's output.

**Relationship** — A connection between two components. May be directional or bidirectional. May be dependency, collaboration, governance, or reference.

**Semantic Space** — The conceptual coordinate system in which every concept has a position defined by its relationships to other concepts.

### 1.4 Atlas Principles

**ATL-P001 — The Navigation Principle.** Every concept in Restaurant OS must be findable through the atlas.

**ATL-P002 — The Relationship Principle.** Every relationship between concepts must be documented in the atlas.

**ATL-P003 — The Currency Principle.** The atlas must reflect the current state of all source documents.

**ATL-P004 — The Entry Principle.** A reader should be able to enter the atlas from any concept and navigate to any related concept.

**ATL-P005 — The Completeness Principle.** The atlas must cover every engine, document, philosophy, and business domain.

**ATL-P006 — The Consistency Principle.** The atlas must be consistent with all source documents.

**ATL-P007 — The Evolution Principle.** The atlas evolves as new engines and documents are added.

**ATL-P008 — The Primacy Principle.** When the atlas and a source document disagree, the source document takes precedence.

## Part 3 — Constitution Dependency Graph

### 3.1 Dependency Types

Every constitutional document in Restaurant OS participates in a dependency network. The following relationship types define this network:

| Relationship | Meaning | Notation |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| **Depends On** | Document A requires Document B to be understood. A references B's concepts. | A → B |
| **Extends** | Document A builds upon Document B's foundation. A would not exist without B. | A ⇒ B |
| **Supersedes** | Document A replaces Document B for a specific domain. B remains for historical reference. | A ⊃ B |
| **References** | Document A mentions Document B but does not depend on it. | A ↔ B |
| **Governs** | Document A sets rules that Document B must obey. | A ▷ B |
| **Is Governed By** | Document A must obey rules set by Document B. | A ◁ B |
| **Aligns With** | Document A is consistent with Document B's philosophy. | A ≡ B |
| **Derives From** | Document A takes its authority from Document B. | A ⇐ B |

```

    Who owns it: Observation System
    What is produced: Observations with confidence, timestamp, source
    What is communicated to: Pattern, Memory, Attention

    Failure mode: Misobservation, missed observation, noise
    Recovery: Re-observation, alternative source

    ↓

Stage 2: Pattern
    What happens: Structure is recognized in observations.
    Who owns it: Pattern System
    What is produced: Patterns with strength, persistence, recurrence
    What is communicated to: Evidence, Knowledge, Memory
    Quality check: Validity, consistency, stability
    Failure mode: False pattern, missed pattern, overfitting

Stage 3: Evidence
    What happens: Patterns are validated or contradicted.
    Who owns it: Evidence System
    What is produced: Evidence with quality, weight, provenance
    What is communicated to: Memory, Knowledge, Reasoning
    Quality check: Accuracy, precision, freshness, completeness
    Failure mode: False evidence, ignored contradiction, weak evidence
    Recovery: Evidence re-collection, alternative sources

    ↓

Stage 4: Memory
    What happens: Validated cognition is retained for future use.
    Who owns it: Memory System
    What is produced: Retained cognition with retention metadata
    What is communicated to: Knowledge, Attention, Reasoning, Decision

    Failure mode: Forgetting, fabrication, corruption

    Failure mode: Misallocation, overload, fragmentation, fatigue

    Who owns it: Reasoning Engine

    Failure mode: False conclusion, overconfidence, underconfidence, bias
    Recovery: Re-reasoning, additional evidence, alternative perspective

    ↓

Stage 8: Decision
    What happens: Commitment to a specific conclusion or course of action.
    Who owns it: Decision Engine
    What is produced: Decisions with rationale, confidence, authority level

Stage 9: Planning

    Who owns it: Planning Engine
    What is produced: Plans with sequences, resources, timelines, contingencies
    What is communicated to: Execution
    Quality check: Feasibility, efficiency, completeness, adaptability
    Failure mode: Impossible plan, incomplete plan, rigid plan
    Recovery: Plan revision, contingency activation, re-planning

    ↓

Stage 10: Execution
    What happens: Plans are carried out in the world.
    Who owns it: Execution Engine
    What is produced: Actions, outcomes, completion confirmation
    What is communicated to: Learning, Observation
    Quality check: Plan adherence, outcome quality, safety
    Failure mode: Execution error, safety violation, incomplete execution
    Recovery: Execution correction, rollback, human takeover

    ↓

Stage 11: Learning
    What happens: Outcomes are analyzed and future cognition is improved.
    Who owns it: Learning Engine
    What is produced: Learnings with confidence, impact, integration
    What is communicated to: Knowledge, Memory, Reasoning
    Quality check: Validity, impact, non-revision of history
    Failure mode: False learning, noise learning, historical revision
    Recovery: Learning reversal, re-learning, alternative analysis

    ↓

Stage 12: Business Pulse
    What happens: Business health is synthesized into a living narrative.
    Who owns it: Business Pulse Engine
    What is produced: Business health narrative with trends, signals, context
    What is communicated to: Owner (through Conversation), all engines
    Quality check: Truthfulness, completeness, timeliness, relevance
    Failure mode: False narrative, missing signal, alarming tone
    Recovery: Narrative revision, signal re-detection, tone correction

    ↓

Stage 13: Owner
    What happens: The owner receives cognition and makes sovereign decisions.
    Who owns it: Owner (human)
    What is produced: Business decisions, strategy, direction
    What is communicated to: Conversation → Reasoning → Decision
    Quality check: Clarity, completeness, respect, calm
    Failure mode: Overload, confusion, anxiety, distrust
    Recovery: Clarification, simplification, human support
```

### 8.2 Information Flow Rules

1. **Forward Flow**: Information flows forward through the pipeline stages. No stage is skipped without justification.

2. **Feedback Flow**: Learning, Reflection, and Business Pulse may feed information backward to earlier stages for improvement.

3. **Bypass Flow**: In emergencies, Observation may communicate directly to Attention, which communicates directly to Reasoning, bypassing Pattern, Evidence, Memory, and Knowledge. The bypass is logged.

4. **Human Flow**: Human input enters through Conversation and may enter at any pipeline stage appropriate to the input.

5. **Meta Flow**: Reflection, Coordination, and Business Pulse observe all pipeline stages but do not change stage outputs.

6. **Quality Flow**: Every stage verifies its output quality before passing to the next stage.

7. **Confidence Flow**: Every stage adds its confidence assessment to the information passing through.

8. **Temporal Flow**: Information is tagged with temporal context — when it was observed, how fresh it is, and when it expires.

9. **Provenance Flow**: Every piece of information carries its source — which engine produced it, and through what path it arrived.

10. **Urgency Flow**: Critical information is escalated through the Attention system, which may bypass standard pipeline stages.

## Part 10 — Restaurant Glass Atlas

### 10.1 Restaurant Glass Philosophy Map

Restaurant Glass is the philosophy governing how the system presents itself to humans. It is not a visual design system — it is a philosophical system that constrains all presentation.

### 10.2 Glass Principles by Domain

| Principle | Domain Application | Engine Constraint | Experience Impact |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Transparency** | All communication, all engines | Must expose cognition; must not hide uncertainty | Humans understand what the system thinks |
| **Calm** | All communication, all engines | Default tone is calm; urgency is exceptional | Humans feel calm, not anxious |
| **Purpose** | Every interaction, every notification | Every communication has a purpose; no noise | Humans are not burdened with unnecessary information |
| **Disappearance** | Background operation, routine cognition | System works in background; only appears when needed | Humans focus on the restaurant, not the system |
| **Non-Competition** | All human interaction | System never competes for human attention | Humans control their focus |

### 10.3 Glass Application Map

| System Aspect | Glass Principle | How Applied |
|---------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Observation** | Disappearance | Observation works silently, never drawing attention to itself |
| **Pattern** | Transparency | Patterns are communicated as observations, not absolute truths |
| **Evidence** | Transparency | Evidence quality and weight are communicated openly |
| **Memory** | Calm | Memory retrieval is seamless, not disruptive |
| **Knowledge** | Honesty | Knowledge confidence is always communicated |
| **Attention** | Non-Competition | System focus and human focus are clearly distinguished |
| **Reasoning** | Transparency | Reasoning paths are explained, not hidden |
| **Decision** | Purpose | Every decision is accompanied by its rationale |
| **Recommendation** | Calm | Recommendations are advisory, not commanding |
| **Conversation** | Warmth | Conversations use natural, warm language |
| **Notification** | Calm | Notifications are batched, prioritised, never alarming |
| **Visual** | Transparency | Visual design is clear, purposeful, never decorative |
| **Motion** | Calm | Motion is ambient, never jarring |
| **Material** | Purpose | Materials communicate function, not decoration |
| **Human Experience** | All Glass Principles | The Human Experience Constitution encodes all Glass principles |

### 10.4 Glass Anti-Patterns by Domain

| Domain | Anti-Pattern | Glass Violation |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Communication** | Corporate, mechanical language | Violates Calm, Warmth |
| **Notification** | Frequent interruptions | Violates Calm, Disappearance |
| **Visual** | Busy, cluttered displays | Violates Calm, Purpose |
| **Motion** | Fast, jarring animations | Violates Calm, Disappearance |
| **Recommendation** | Commands presented as advice | Violates Calm, Non-Competition |
| **Conversation** | Robotic, impersonal responses | Violates Warmth, Transparency |
| **Feedback** | Scoring, grading, ranking | Violates Calm, Employee Growth |
| **Error** | Blaming, technical language | Violates Calm, Trust |

### 12.2 Pipeline Stage Map

| Stage | Engine | Input From | Output To | Function |
|-------|--------|------------|-----------|----------|

| 4 — Memory | Memory System | Observation, Pattern, Evidence, Knowledge | Knowledge, Attention, Reasoning | Retain |
| 5 — Knowledge | Knowledge System | Observation, Pattern, Evidence, Memory, Learning | Attention, Reasoning, Decision | Understand |
| 6 — Attention Allocation | Attention System | Observation, Pattern, Memory, Knowledge | Reasoning, Decision, Human Experience | Focus |
| 7 — Reasoning | Reasoning Engine | Attention, Knowledge, Memory, Evidence | Decision, Recommendation, Business Pulse | Think |
| 8 — Decision | Decision Engine | Reasoning, Knowledge, Memory, Prediction | Planning, Execution, Human Experience | Commit |
| 9 — Planning | Planning Engine | Decision, Knowledge, Memory, Prediction | Execution | Sequence |
| 10 — Execution | Execution Engine | Decision, Planning | Learning, Observation | Act |
| 11 — Learning | Learning Engine | Execution, Outcome, Observation | Knowledge, Memory, Reasoning | Improve |
| 12 — Business Pulse | Business Pulse Engine | Observation, Knowledge, Reasoning, All | Owner (via Conversation), All | Narrate |

### 12.3 Pipeline Branches

| Branch | Path | Purpose | When Used |
|--------|------|---------|-----------|
| **Emergency** | Obs → Attn → Reas → Dec → Exec | Rapid response to safety-critical situations | Safety emergencies |
| **Direct Memory Retrieval** | Mem → Reas | Historical context for reasoning without attention pre-processing | When reasoning needs specific history |
| **Learning Feedback** | Exec → Learn → Know → Reas | Continuous improvement cycle | After any execution |
| **Business Narrative** | Obs + Know + Reas → BP → Conv | Business health reporting | Daily, weekly, on request |
| **Human Input** | Human → Conv → Reas → Dec | Sovereign human override | Owner decisions |
| **Recommendation** | Reas → Rec → Conv → Human | Advisory communication | When system has a suggestion |
| **Meta Review** | All → Refl → All | Cognitive system improvement | Periodic, on trigger |
| **Prediction Support** | Know + Pat + Mem → Pred → Reas + Dec | Future state estimation | Planning, forecasting |

### 12.4 Pipeline Feedback Loops

| Loop | Path | Effect |
|------|------|--------|
| **Learning** | Outcome → Learning → Knowledge → Reasoning → Decision → Planning → Execution | Improves future cognition from outcomes |
| **Confidence** | Execution → Observation → Evidence → Reasoning → Confidence | Improves confidence calibration |
| **Correction** | Observation → Evidence → Reasoning → Decision (revision) | Revises decisions based on new observations |
| **Evolution** | Reflection → All → Evolution | Improves engines over time |

### 12.5 Pipeline Recovery Paths

| Failure Point | Recovery Path | Degraded Mode |
|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| Observation fails | Re-observe or use alternative source | Reduced accuracy, increased uncertainty |
| Pattern fails | Extend observation period, re-evaluate | Reduced pattern recognition |
| Evidence fails | Re-collect or find alternative sources | Reduced validation confidence |
| Memory fails | Re-store or reconstruct from other engines | Reduced context, increased recency bias |
| Knowledge fails | Re-validate or seek new evidence | Reduced understanding confidence |
| Attention fails | Re-prioritise, shed load, restore focus | Degraded prioritisation |
| Reasoning fails | Re-reason with additional evidence or alternative methods | Reduced conclusion quality |
| Decision fails | Re-decide or escalate to human | Delayed action, increased uncertainty |
| Planning fails | Re-plan with adjusted constraints | Suboptimal sequences |
| Execution fails | Re-execute or roll back | Incomplete action, safety mode |

## Part 18 — Cognitive Layer Map

### 18.1 Layer Definitions

Restaurant OS cognition operates across multiple layers:

| Layer | Function | Examples | Governed By | Quality Criteria |
|-------|----------|----------|-------------|------------------|
| **Perception Layer** | Sensing reality, capturing signals | Observation | Cognitive Architecture | Accuracy, Completeness, Timeliness |
| **Understanding Layer** | Structuring, validating, integrating | Pattern, Evidence, Knowledge | Cognitive Architecture | Validity, Consistency, Coherence |
| **Storage Layer** | Retaining, organizing, retrieving | Memory | Cognitive Architecture | Integrity, Retrieval Accuracy, Organization |
| **Allocation Layer** | Prioritizing, focusing, load balancing | Attention | Cognitive Architecture, Human Experience | Relevance, Timeliness, Calm |
| **Processing Layer** | Reasoning, deciding, planning, predicting | Reasoning, Decision, Planning, Prediction | Engine Contract | Soundness, Alignment, Feasibility, Accuracy |
| **Expression Layer** | Communicating, recommending, conversing | Recommendation, Conversation | Human Experience Constitution | Clarity, Warmth, Relevance |
| **Action Layer** | Executing, coordinating | Execution, Coordination | Engine Contract, Constitution | Safety, Plan Adherence, Reliability |
| **Meta Layer** | Learning, reflecting, monitoring | Learning, Reflection, Business Pulse | Engine Contract, Human Experience | Validity, Impact, Honesty |
| **Constitutional Layer** | Governing, aligning, constraining | Human Experience, Constitution | Constitution (supreme) | Alignment, Consistency, Completeness |

### 18.2 Layer Interaction Rules

1. **Lower layers serve higher layers** — Perception serves Understanding, which serves Processing, which serves Expression.

2. **Higher layers may request from lower layers** — Reasoning may request additional Evidence. Decision may request Memory context.

3. **Constitutional layers constrain all layers** — Human Experience Constitution constrains every layer's output.

4. **Meta layers observe all layers** — Reflection, Learning, and Business Pulse observe without interfering.

5. **Storage layers serve all layers** — Memory provides context to any layer that requires it.

### 18.3 Layer Communication Constraints

| Sender Layer | Receiver Layer | Allowed | Constraint |
|-------------|---------------|---------|------------|
| Perception | Understanding | Yes | Must pass quality threshold |
| Perception | Storage | Yes | Only significant signals stored |
| Understanding | Processing | Yes | Must include confidence |
| Understanding | Storage | Yes | Only validated knowledge stored |
| Processing | Expression | Yes | Must use advisory language |
| Processing | Action | Yes | Must include safety checks |
| Meta | All | Yes (observation only) | Must not interfere |
| Constitutional | All | Yes (governance) | Must be principle-based |
| Expression | Human | Yes | Must be calm, warm, clear |
| Human | Expression | Yes | Must preserve sovereignty |
| Action | Perception | Yes (feedback) | Must not create perception noise |

### 18.4 Layer Failure Isolation

Failures in one layer must be isolated to prevent cascading:

| Failing Layer | Impact on Same Layer | Impact on Higher Layers | Impact on Lower Layers | Recovery Strategy |

## Part 30 — Constitution of the Atlas

### 30.1 Atlas Laws

**ATLAS-LAW-001 — Navigation Completeness.** Every document, engine, philosophy, and business concept in Restaurant OS must appear in the Cognitive Atlas.

**ATLAS-LAW-002 — Cross-Reference Completeness.** Every document must be cross-referenced with every related document in the Atlas.

**ATLAS-LAW-003 — Dependency Accuracy.** Every dependency recorded in the Atlas must be verified against the source documents.

**ATLAS-LAW-004 — Currency.** The Atlas must be updated whenever a source document changes.

**ATLAS-LAW-005 — Primacy.** When the Atlas and a source document disagree, the source document takes precedence.

**ATLAS-LAW-006 — Entry.** A reader should be able to enter the Atlas from any concept and navigate to any related concept.

**ATLAS-LAW-007 — Evolution.** The Atlas evolves as the cognitive system evolves. Each new engine or document requires an Atlas update.

**ATLAS-LAW-008 — Audit.** The Atlas is verified during each constitutional audit.

**ATLAS-LAW-009 — No Orphans.** Every concept must have at least one relationship to another concept. No isolated concepts.

**ATLAS-LAW-010 — Semantic Consistency.** Every concept must have the same meaning across all Atlas entries.

**ATLAS-LAW-011 — Principle Alignment.** Every navigation path must align with the principles defined in the Constitution, Human Experience Constitution, and Engine Contract.

**ATLAS-LAW-012 — Engine Parity.** Every engine defined in the Engine Contract must appear in the Atlas. No engine is invisible to navigation.

**ATLAS-LAW-013 — Bilaterality.** Every relationship in the Atlas must be bidirectional. If A depends on B, the Atlas must show both A depends on B and B is depended upon by A.

**ATLAS-LAW-014 — Dimension Parity.** All navigation dimensions must provide equal depth. No dimension may be a stub while others are complete.

**ATLAS-LAW-015 — No Dead Ends.** Every navigation path must lead to at least one other concept. No cul-de-sacs in the semantic space.

**ATLAS-LAW-016 — Document Scope.** Every document's scope must be clearly defined in the Atlas. Ambiguous boundaries are not permitted.

**ATLAS-LAW-017 — Engine Contract Completeness.** Every engine's contract — accepts, rejects, produces, cannot modify, assumes, never assumes — must be recorded in the Atlas.

**ATLAS-LAW-018 — Boundary Clarity.** Every engine boundary recorded in the Atlas must match its boundary defined in its source contract.

**ATLAS-LAW-019 — Quality Linkage.** Every engine's quality dimensions in the Atlas must match its quality dimensions in its source document.

**ATLAS-LAW-020 — Failure Mapping.** Every engine's failure modes in the Atlas must be complete based on its source document.

**ATLAS-LAW-021 — Event Coverage.** Every business event mapped in the Atlas must include trigger, flow, engines involved, duration, and communication type.

**ATLAS-LAW-022 — Role Empathy.** Every role mapped in the Atlas must include emotional goals and states to avoid, per the Human Experience Constitution.

**ATLAS-LAW-023 — Scenario Completeness.** The Atlas must include scenarios covering normal operations, peak operations, crisis operations, and strategic operations.

**ATLAS-LAW-024 — Future Readiness.** The Atlas must reserve space for future engines, documents, concepts, and relationships.

**ATLAS-LAW-025 — Audit Trail.** Every update to the Atlas must be recorded with date, scope of change, and verifying agent.

### 30.2 Atlas Obligations

Every agent and human who maintains the Atlas accepts the following obligations:

**ATLAS-OBL-001 — Fidelity.** The Atlas must faithfully represent every source document. Creative interpretation is not permitted.

**ATLAS-OBL-002 — Diligence.** The Atlas maintainer must verify every relationship against source documents before recording it.

**ATLAS-OBL-003 — Humility.** When the Atlas maintainer discovers a relationship that seems incorrect but is confirmed by the source documents, the Atlas must record the relationship, not correct it.

**ATLAS-OBL-004 — Timeliness.** The Atlas must be updated within one working cycle of any source document change.

**ATLAS-OBL-005 — Completeness.** The Atlas maintainer must ensure every source document is represented in the Atlas before declaring the Atlas current.

### 30.3 Atlas Governance

| Governance Principle | Application |
|---------------------|-------------|
| Atlas Authority | The Atlas is authoritative for navigation only. Source documents are authoritative for content. |
| Atlas Primacy | For navigation questions, the Atlas takes precedence over any individual document's table of contents. |
| Atlas Review | The Atlas is reviewed during every constitutional audit. |
| Atlas Amendment | The Atlas is amended by adding new entries. Existing entries are corrected, not overwritten. |
