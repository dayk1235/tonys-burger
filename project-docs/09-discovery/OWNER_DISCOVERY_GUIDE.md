# OWNER DISCOVERY GUIDE — First Owner Feedback Session

**Date:** 2026-06-14
**Status:** Pre-session preparation
**Purpose:** Prepare the first structured interview with the business owner to collect real business data, validate the website, and lay the groundwork for TonyBot MVP.

---

## 1. Website Feedback

Begin with open-ended questions. Let the owner lead. Take notes verbatim.

### Questions

- What do you like about the site?
- What feels wrong or uncomfortable?
- Does this represent your business accurately?
- What would you change first?
- Would you show this to a customer right now? Why or why not?
- Does the menu reflect what you actually sell?
- Do the prices match your real prices?
- Does the location/hours information look correct?

### Interview Flow

1. Open the site together on the owner's phone and a laptop
2. Let them scroll freely without prompting — record their natural reactions
3. Ask open-ended questions first, then specific ones
4. Do NOT defend design decisions. Listen.

### Expected Outcomes

- 3–10 change requests from owner
- Validated (or invalidated) brand direction
- Trust established — owner sees you listening
- Priority list of what matters most to the business

## 3. POS Discovery

### Questions

- Which POS system do you use? (e.g., Clip, Square, iZettle, POSitive, custom)
- Can it export reports to PDF?
- Can it export to Excel?
- Can it export to CSV?
- What reports do you use most? (e.g., daily sales, product popularity, shift summaries)
- How do you currently track inventory?
- Do you use the POS for employee management or just sales?
- Who installed or configured your POS?

### Interview Flow

1. Ask them to open their POS system on their phone or tablet
2. Ask them to navigate to the reports or export section
3. If possible, take a screen recording of where reports live
4. Ask what they WISH the POS could do

### Expected Outcomes

- POS name and version documented
- Export capabilities confirmed (PDF/Excel/CSV)
- List of most-used reports
- Inventory tracking method understood
- Gap analysis: what POS cannot do that owner needs

---

## 4. Sales Discovery

### Questions

- What are your top 5 best-selling products?
- Which combo sells the most?
- What are your peak hours? (day of week, time of day)
- What promotions have worked best in the past?
- What are the most common questions customers ask before ordering?
- What is your average ticket / average order value?
- Do you have any seasonal items or limited-time offers?
- What items have the highest and lowest margins?

### Interview Flow

1. Start with easy questions (top products) and move to harder ones (margins)
2. Use their POS or memory for top product names
3. Write down exact names as the owner says them (brand names, cuts, etc.)
4. Ask for specific numbers where possible: "Roughly how many per week?"

### Expected Outcomes

- Top 5 products with rough volume estimates
- Peak hours and days identified
- Best promotions documented
- Frequent customer questions captured (raw material for TonyBot)
- Average order value estimated

## 5. Digital Presence

### Questions

- Do you have a Facebook page? Who manages it?
- How do you use WhatsApp for orders? (individual chat, broadcast list, business API?)
- Do you use delivery apps? (Uber Eats, Didi Food, Rappi, SinDelantal?)
- Have you done any paid ads? Where? What results?
- Do you have an email list or SMS list?
- How do customers usually find you? (walk-in, referral, social, delivery app)
- Who takes photos of the food currently?
- Have you ever run a contest, giveaway, or loyalty program?

### Interview Flow

1. Ask the owner to open their Facebook/Instagram business page
2. Look at posting frequency, engagement, best-performing posts
3. Check the WhatsApp Business profile if applicable
4. Ask about monthly spend on ads/delivery commissions

### Expected Outcomes

- List of active digital channels with engagement levels
- Understanding of WhatsApp ordering workflow (manual vs. semi-automated)
- Delivery app partnerships and commission costs
- Current marketing effectiveness (or lack thereof)
- Photo quality standards understood

## 6. Future Opportunities

### Questions

- What part of your day takes the most time?
- What repetitive questions do your customers ask? (write down exact wordings)
- What would you automate if you could?
- What frustrates you most about running the business?
- Would you like customers to be able to check the menu online without calling?
- Would you like a way for customers to see wait times before ordering?
- If you had a magic button that did one thing for your business, what would it do?
- Have you ever thought about taking pre-orders or catering orders?
- What would make you excited to use a dashboard every morning?

### Interview Flow

1. This section comes last — the owner is warmed up and thinking about possibilities
2. Do NOT pitch TonyBot. Let the owner describe their pain points first.
3. If they describe a problem that TonyBot solves, note it silently
4. End by asking: "If we could fix ONE thing, what would it be?"

### Expected Outcomes

- Top 3 operational pain points identified
- List of repetitive customer questions (raw training data for TonyBot)
- Automation appetite understood (high/medium/low)
- Owner's single biggest priority captured
- TonyBot MVP scope informed by real needs, not assumptions

## Information Needed Before TonyBot MVP

The following must be collected before Phase 5 (TonyBot MVP) can begin:

1. **Real menu** — names, descriptions, prices, categories, dietary tags
2. **Real hours** — day-by-day open/close times
3. **Real address** — exact location for directions/maps
4. **Real WhatsApp number** — verified, with business profile
5. **Top 5 products** — names and rough volume
6. **Peak hours** — busiest times for staffing/promotions
7. **Frequent customer questions** — 10–20 exact wordings (QA training data)
8. **POS export capability** — determines automated data pipeline feasibility
9. **Delivery app partnerships** — menu parity and commission awareness
10. **Logo file** — vector preferred, high-res PNG minimum

### Minimum Viable Data Set (can start TonyBot without?)

| Data | Required for MVP? | Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Real menu + prices | Yes | TonyBot must answer "How much is X?" |
| Real hours | Yes | TonyBot must answer "Are you open?" |
| Real WhatsApp number | Yes | TonyBot must hand off orders |
| Top 5 products | Yes | Knowledge base priority ordering |
| Frequent questions | Yes | QA pairs for response generation |
| Logo | No | Cosmetic — can add later |
| Real photos | No | Cosmetic — can add later |
| POS export | No | Phase 6 automation |
| Delivery apps | No | Phase 6 data enrichment |

## Recommendation: Proceed to Owner Meeting?

**YES** — with the following notes:

### Prerequisites Met
- Website is functional and presentable (Demo Readiness Score: 58/100)
- All core sections render correctly
- Spanish copy is final
- CTA works and fires analytics
- Owner's feedback is the last validation step before production preparation

### Red Flags to Watch For
- Owner says "I don't have time" → reschedule shorter session (30 min)
- Owner says "It looks good, I have no changes" → push harder on Section 1
- Owner cannot provide basic data (menu, hours, WhatsApp) → delay TonyBot, focus on data collection
- Owner is confused by the site → note all confusion points for UX fixes

### Preparation Required Before Meeting
- [ ] Print or load this guide
- [ ] Ensure site loads on both desktop and mobile (use owner's phone)
- [ ] Have WhatsApp Business profile ready for verification
- [ ] Test all links (WhatsApp, social, navigation) before session
- [ ] Prepare a simple "thank you" follow-up (coffee or small gesture recommended)
- [ ] Confirm meeting space has WiFi (or use hotspot)

### Post-Session Actions
- Replace all placeholder data within 48 hours
- Capture any bugs reported by owner as new tasks
- Document owner's feedback verbatim in `project-docs/09-discovery/OWNER_FEEDBACK.md`
- Re-run Demo Readiness Audit with real data
- If TonyBot MVP is confirmed, begin Phase 5 planning
